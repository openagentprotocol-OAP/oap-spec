# Security Policy

## Reporting a vulnerability

Responsible disclosure is essential to the integrity of the Open Agent Protocol ecosystem. If you discover a vulnerability in the specification, the reference implementations, or any Foundation operated service, please report it through one of the following channels.

1. **Email.** `security@openagentprotocol.eu` (PGP key published at `https://openagentprotocol.eu/.well-known/pgp-key.asc`).
2. **Foundation portal.** `https://openagentprotocol.eu/security/report` with optional anonymous submission.
3. **In person.** At Foundation working group meetings, to any Board member.

## What to include

1. A description of the vulnerability and its impact.
2. Steps to reproduce.
3. Affected versions of the specification or reference implementations.
4. Any proof of concept code, screenshots, or logs.
5. Your preferred attribution and contact details (or your wish to remain anonymous).

## Process

1. Acknowledgment within seventy two hours.
2. Initial assessment within seven days.
3. Coordinated disclosure timeline agreed with the reporter, typically within ninety days.
4. Public advisory and fix release through the canonical channels.
5. Credit in the advisory unless the reporter requests otherwise.

## Bounties

The Foundation operates a Vulnerability Bounty Program funded from Foundation reserves. Bounty levels are published at `https://openagentprotocol.eu/security/bounty`.

## Out of scope

1. Vulnerabilities in third party Tools that are not Foundation operated. Report these directly to the Tool publisher and, if material, to the Foundation Trust Service.
2. Theoretical attacks without a practical proof of concept.
3. Attacks requiring physical access to a target device.

## Whistleblower protection

Reports of Manifest fraud, ethics violations, or governance misconduct are routed through the Foundation Whistleblower Program with cryptographic anonymity guarantees and statutory anti retaliation protections in supported jurisdictions.
