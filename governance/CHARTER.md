# Open Agent Protocol Foundation Charter

**Document Identifier:** OAP-CHARTER-1.0
**Status:** Public Working Draft
**Date:** 2026-05-03

## 1. Mission

The Open Agent Protocol Foundation is a neutral non profit organization that maintains the Open Agent Protocol specification, operates the trust anchoring services that the protocol depends on, and adjudicates disputes that participants cannot resolve directly.

## 2. Principles

1. **Neutrality.** No single corporate participant may dictate the direction of the specification.
2. **Openness.** All specification work is public. All decisions are documented.
3. **Multi stakeholder governance.** Industry, civil society, academia, and government observers are represented.
4. **Public interest.** Where commercial and public interests conflict, the Foundation defers to the public interest.

## 3. Membership

Membership is open to any organization or individual that supports the mission. Membership classes are:

| Class | Eligibility | Voting weight | Annual dues |
|-------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| Individual | Any natural person. | One vote in working groups. | None. |
| Non Profit | Registered non profit organization. | One Board seat candidacy. | Waived. |
| Academic | Recognized academic institution. | One Board seat candidacy. | Waived. |
| Industry, Small | Up to fifty employees. | Working group voting. | Tiered. |
| Industry, Mid | Fifty to one thousand employees. | Working group voting. | Tiered. |
| Industry, Large | Over one thousand employees. | Working group and Board candidacy. | Tiered. |
| Government Observer | National regulator or supranational body. | Non voting observer. | Waived. |

No single member may hold more than fifteen percent of voting weight in any organ of the Foundation.

## 4. Board of Directors

The Board has thirteen seats:

1. Three Industry seats (one Small, one Mid, one Large).
2. Two Civil Society seats (consumer protection, digital rights).
3. Two Academic seats.
4. Two Practitioner seats (one legal, one medical or accessibility).
5. One Labor seat.
6. One Independent Chair elected by the Board.
7. Two At Large seats elected by the full membership.

Board terms are two years, staggered, with a maximum of two consecutive terms.

## 5. Working Groups

Standing Working Groups include:

1. Core Protocol.
2. Confidentiality and Compliance.
3. Wallet and Settlement.
4. Trust and Reputation.
5. Marketplace and Discovery.
6. Accessibility.
7. Compliance Mapping.
8. Reference Implementations.

Each Working Group elects a Chair from its members and reports to the Board quarterly.

## 6. Ethics Board

The Ethics Board is independent of the Board of Directors and consists of seven members appointed for three year terms:

1. Two ethicists.
2. One human rights lawyer.
3. One medical practitioner or bioethicist.
4. One accessibility advocate.
5. One labor representative.
6. One representative of vulnerable populations advocacy.

The Ethics Board approves changes to the L1 Universal Prohibitions, reviews quarterly conformance reports, and may issue binding guidance to the Foundation Board.

## 7. Foundation Services

### 7.1 Mandatory

1. Specification stewardship and the OAP RFC process.
2. Transparency Log operation in at least three independent geographic regions.
3. Revocation Service.
4. DID Resolver caching layer.
5. Trust Score computation and publication.
6. Sampling Service for Outcome Verification.
7. Dispute Resolution Service.
8. Whistleblower intake.
9. Compliance Working Group output.
10. Accessibility conformance program.

### 7.2 Optional

1. Insurance Pool through a licensed carrier partner.
2. Reference Marketplace.
3. Reference Wallet (operated under appropriate financial regulation).
4. Reference Agent runtime.

## 8. Funding

1. Settlement fee of fifty basis points on transactions traversing a Foundation operated Wallet.
2. Member dues per the schedule in Section 3.
3. Public research grants.
4. Donations subject to the Conflict of Interest Policy.

Foundation budgets and audited financial statements are published annually.

## 9. Anti Capture Provisions

1. Marketplace ranking algorithms operated by Foundation members are open source.
2. Foundation services operate under a public service charter with binding non discrimination obligations.
3. The Foundation has no veto over Marketplace listings beyond the L1 Universal Prohibitions and validated Manifest non conformance.
4. Conformance Level credentials are revocable and appealable through the Dispute Resolution Service.
5. Multiple competing Marketplaces are presumed and encouraged.
6. Board members disclose all material affiliations and recuse from votes presenting a conflict.

## 10. Amendments

This Charter may be amended by a two thirds vote of the Board, ratified by a majority of voting members within sixty days, after a public comment period of at least thirty days.
